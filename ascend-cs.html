My work at Ascend

Responsive design and build

In today’s digital landscape, users access websites from a wide range of devices, including desktops, tablets, and smartphones. Responsive design ensures that a website adapts seamlessly to different screen sizes, providing an optimal viewing and interaction experience. This approach involves flexible grids, scalable images, and CSS media queries to maintain usability and visual consistency across all devices. A well-executed responsive design improves user experience, boosts engagement, and enhances accessibility.

The development process for responsive websites involves building a fluid layout that dynamically adjusts to different screen resolutions. Testing across multiple devices and browsers ensures that performance, functionality, and aesthetics remain consistent. Ultimately, a responsive design and build strategy leads to a more accessible and future-proof website, benefiting both users and businesses alike.

The job

I redesigned Ascend’s website to create a more modern, professional, and user-friendly experience. The old site felt outdated and lacked visual appeal, so I introduced a refreshed color scheme, improved typography, and a cleaner layout to enhance readability and navigation. My goal was to ensure the site felt intuitive and engaging.

The site was designed by myself in Figma and built using Webflow, leveraging its powerful design tools to craft a sleek, responsive layout. Due to Webflow’s free plan limitations, the project was restricted to a couple pages and wasn’t published before my departure from Ascend. However, this redesign serves as a strong example of modern web design principles and can easily be expanded into a full-scale site. The project showcases my ability to transform outdated websites into polished, conversion-friendly platforms, making it a great template for future builds.
